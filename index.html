Churn Attribution Across Drivers

A multi-factor attribution model, same logic as Markov chain multi-touch attribution, but allocating credit for churn across account and product factors instead of marketing touchpoints. Standardized logistic regression, credit normalized to 100%. Model AUC = 0.863. Note: the four individual QoE telemetry columns (rebuffering, startup, video, audio) were excluded from this model because they carry 0.99 correlation with each other in this synthetic layer, splitting credit across them would be statistically meaningless, so they’re rolled up into one aggregate QoE score here instead.
5 Real Factors + 1 Modeled Factor
Removal-effect check (same idea as removing a touchpoint in a Markov chain model): dropping QoE from the model costs 0.057 AUC, more than five times the next-largest drop (Tenure, 0.011). QoE isn’t just correlated with churn; removing it measurably weakens the model’s ability to predict it.
